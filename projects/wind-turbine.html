Overview

This project was undertaken through a student-led engineering society, aiming to design and build a full horizontal-axis wind turbine (HAWT) from the ground up. The blades are among the most critical components, requiring careful attention to aerofoil geometry, structural integrity, and manufacturing precision.

⚙️ Delivered blades that largely met the aerodynamic and structural performance targets for the 750 W turbine system.

Role & Leadership

As Blades Sub-Team Lead, responsibility extended beyond personal technical contributions to managing a team of five engineers and coordinating all aspects of blade design and manufacture.

  • Managed a sub-team of five engineers, allocating tasks and tracking progress against milestones
  • Coordinated weekly team meetings and facilitated academic-led training sessions on workshop safety and technique
  • Prepared and submitted risk assessments, COSHH documentation, and purchasing requisitions for materials and consumables
  • Acted as the primary interface between the blades sub-team and the wider turbine project team

Design & Manufacturing

The blade design was based on a provided manufacturing recipe and technical drawings, which were interpreted and adapted to suit the available workshop resources. The aerofoil profiles were translated into a detailed 3D model in Fusion 360, which served as the reference geometry throughout manufacture.

  • Created a full 3D blade model in Fusion 360 from the provided technical drawings, enabling visualisation and measurement verification
  • Led hand-shaped manufacture of wooden blades using hand planes, belt sanders, and jigsaws
  • Developed a systematic sanding and shaping sequence to progressively achieve the target aerofoil cross-section
  • Adapted the manufacturing approach iteratively based on material behaviour and tooling availability

Outcome

The completed blades achieved dimensions closely matching the design intent and met most of the prescribed performance requirements. The project provided extensive hands-on experience in manufacturing planning, team coordination, and translating design intent into physical components under real constraints.
