Overview

The competition brief required teams to design a custom obstacle course and then programme a robotic arm to navigate it autonomously. The challenge tested both mechanical design skills — in creating obstacles that demanded precision and adaptability — and robotics programming capabilities.

✅ Successfully demonstrated the full system to the judging panel, receiving positive feedback on both the obstacle design and robot performance.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Served as Team Leader, facilitating team discussions and driving the project from concept through to live demonstration
  • Coordinated task allocation across team members, ensuring design, programming, and testing workstreams progressed in parallel
  • Designed obstacle elements using 3D modelling, creating features that challenged precision positioning and path planning
  • Oversaw integration of the obstacle design with the robot's programmed motion sequences

Technical Execution

The project utilised a 6-axis Universal Robots (UR) collaborative robotic arm. The obstacle course was designed to require careful end-effector path planning, incorporating features such as narrow passages, height changes, and objects requiring precise placement.

3D models of the obstacles were produced and used to verify clearances and plan joint trajectories prior to physical testing. The team iteratively refined both the obstacle geometry and the robot's programmed paths during testing phases to achieve reliable, repeatable performance.

Outcome

The demonstration was completed successfully, with the robotic arm navigating all course elements within the allotted time. Judges provided positive feedback on the clarity of the team's approach, the thoughtful obstacle design, and the smoothness of the robot's executed movements.
