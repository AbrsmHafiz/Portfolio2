Electric Bicycle Gearbox

Overview

The objective of this project was to develop a 250 W electric-assisted pedal cycle by designing and building a custom gearbox from scratch, using acrylic as the primary structural material. The project combined mechanical design, electronics integration, and rapid prototyping to produce a lightweight, fully functional system capable of delivering smooth power assistance.

🏆 Achieved top 10% distance performance in the 30-second competitive test, demonstrating effective power transmission and drivetrain efficiency.

Design & Development

The design process began with defining the power requirements and selecting an appropriate gear ratio to match the 250 W motor's torque output to the bicycle's drivetrain. Fusion 360 was used throughout to create detailed 3D models of the gearbox housing, gears, and mounting interfaces.

Acrylic was selected as the casing material due to its balance of machinability, weight, and availability for laser cutting. The design went through several iterations to optimise gear meshing and reduce friction losses.

Key Contributions

  • Designed and built a 250 W gearbox converting a standard bicycle into an electric-assisted cycle capable of 15.5 mph
  • Utilised Fusion 360 for full 3D mechanical design, with Arduino programming for electronic control
  • Applied 3D printing and laser cutting for rapid prototyping and production of final components
  • Conducted Finite Element Analysis (FEA) on the laser-cut acrylic casing, identifying stress concentrations and proposing geometry changes
  • Design changes resulting from FEA reduced material use by 30% while maintaining full structural integrity
  • Integrated electronic control systems with the mechanical drivetrain for seamless, responsive power delivery

Engineering Analysis

FEA was performed in Fusion 360 to evaluate the structural behaviour of the acrylic casing under expected loading conditions. The analysis identified regions of elevated stress around mounting points and gear shaft bores. By redistributing material through ribbing and corner radii optimisation, a 30% reduction in material was achieved without compromising the safety factor.

The electronic control system used an Arduino microcontroller to modulate motor power based on pedalling input, ensuring smooth power delivery across different operating conditions.
