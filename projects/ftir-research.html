Summer Research

Lubricant Detection
Using FTIR

Independently evaluated Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy as a detection method for top-of-rail friction modifiers applied at industry-relevant thicknesses.

Type
Independent Research
Technique
FTIR Spectroscopy
Tools
Tribometer, Wheel–Rail Rig, Excel
Outcome
Reliable Detection Confirmed

Research Objective

Top-of-rail (TOR) friction modifiers are applied to rail tracks to reduce wear, noise, and energy consumption. Reliable detection and characterisation of TOR products is critical for understanding their performance and managing application. This project investigated whether FTIR spectroscopy could serve as a practical, sensitive detection method for TOR friction modifiers at realistic application thicknesses.

🔬 Successfully demonstrated reliable FTIR detection of top-of-rail friction modifiers at three distinct low application thicknesses.

Experimental Approach

The research was conducted independently, encompassing sample preparation, experimental testing, and data analysis. Real rail samples were used throughout to ensure relevance to operational conditions.

  • Applied TOR friction modifiers to real rail samples at three controlled low application thicknesses using a scaled wheel–rail test rig
  • Conducted FTIR spectroscopy on treated and untreated rail samples to generate absorption spectra
  • Performed tribometry tests to characterise the friction characteristics associated with each application level
  • Processed and analysed spectral data in Excel, focusing on identification and quantification of characteristic absorption peaks

Findings

Analysis of the FTIR spectra confirmed that characteristic absorption peaks associated with the friction modifier compounds were detectable at all three application thicknesses tested. The signal-to-noise ratio was sufficient for reliable identification, supporting the feasibility of FTIR as a monitoring tool in rail maintenance contexts.

The tribometry data provided corroborating evidence that the applied modifier quantities were functionally significant, reinforcing the relevance of detection at those thicknesses.
